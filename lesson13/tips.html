Tips

1. Book Early

  • Spots on rafting trips tend to fill up fast, so if you want to get a tour with your preferred length and difficulty, it’s best to book as early as possible.

2. Wear a Life Jacket and Helmet at All Times

  • Regardless of the difficulty level of your rafting course, wear a helmet at all times.
  • Outer layer of windproof and waterproof jacket & pants

3. Listen to Your Guide

  • Set of dry clothes, shoes and socks – warmer gear if cool temperatures after trip Towel
  • Bag for a wet clothes

4. Don’t Bring Too Many Electronics

  • Rafting adventures are great opportunities to enjoy the company of friends and the beauty of nature and the sounds of the forest.

5.Be Ready for the Unexpected

  • You must expect the unexpected at all times because nothing can fully prepare you for a rafting trip.

Summary Of River Classifications

Class I – Easy.Very small rough areas perfect for absolute beginners, few obstructions. Risk to swimmers is slight; self-rescue is easy (skill level: none).

Class II – Novice. Some rough water, good for novices. Occasional maneuvering may be required. Medium-sized waves are easily avoided by trained paddlers (skill level: basic paddling).

Class III – Intermediate. Medium waves or strainers may be present but not much considerable danger. Complex maneuvering are often required (skill level: experienced paddling).

Class IV – Advanced. Large waves, long rapids, rocks, maybe a considerable drop, sharp maneuvers may be needed. Rapids may require "must make" moves above dangerous hazards (skill level: advanced whitewater experience).

Class V – Expert. Extremely long obstructed rapids, failure to execute a specific maneuver at a specific point may result in serious injury or death. Proper equipment, extensive experience, and practiced rescue skills are essential (skill level: expert).

Class VI– Extreme and Exploratory Rapids hat are not passable and any attempt to do so would result in serious injury, near drowning, or death. The consequences of errors are severe and rescue may be impossible.
